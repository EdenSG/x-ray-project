What are X-Rays?

X-rays are a form of electromagnetic radiation, classified as having a wavelength from 0.01nm to 10nm, or frequencies from 30 petahertz to 30 exahertz (3×1016 Hz to 3×1019 Hz). X-rays are used for medical scanning because softer tissues are transparent to X-rays, whereas more dense tissues such as bone are opaque. There are many types of medical X-ray procedures, including projectional radiography, fluoroscopy, computed tomography, and mammography. I will be focusing on projectional radiography, where a two-dimensional image is formed on a film sensitive to x-rays or more recently on CCD sensors designed to detect x-rays.
